Dataset

The Diabetes prediction dataset is a collection of medical and demographic data from patients, along with their diabetes status (positive or negative). The data includes features such as age, gender, body mass index (BMI), hypertension, heart disease, smoking history, HbA1c level, and blood glucose level. This dataset can be used to build machine learning models to predict diabetes in patients based on their medical history and demographic information. This can be useful for healthcare professionals in identifying patients who may be at risk of developing diabetes and in developing personalized treatment plans. Additionally, the dataset can be used by researchers to explore the relationships between various medical and demographic factors and the likelihood of developing diabetes. The features and label

The dataset contains eight feaures and one label excluding the column 'ID'. The features include:

  • Gender: takes up either Male or Female
  • Age: The age of the individual
  • Hypertension: Denotes whether the individual has hypertension (denoted by 1) or not (denoted by 0)
  • Heart disease: Denotes whether the individual has a heart disease (denoted by 1) or not (denoted by 0)
  • Smoking history: A category that best describes the smoking habits of the individual.
  • BMI: Body Mass Index of the individual
  • HbA1c level: a simple blood test that measures your average blood sugar levels over the past 3 months
  • Blood glucose level: The level of glucose (sugar) in the individuals blood

The label is the diabetes column and this is the column that is meant to be predicted. The column is denoted by 1 if the individual has diabetes and 0 if the individual does not have diabetes. The prediction can be done by analysisng the features and understanding the relationship between the features and the label and how a small or major change in one of the features effect the probability of the label taking up either of its possible values.

The detailed analysis of each of these features and the effect it has on the label can be found in github.
